Clustering of football events

Clustering of football events The goal is to cluster events (f.ex. position), to detect if there is a difference amongst them. For example:

  • are there different types of defenders depending on their position on the field?
  • are there different types of teams depending on their eventtypes during a match?
  • etc.
By not fixing the number of clusters in advance and leaving it up to the user, maximal opportunity is created to let the user detect patterns himself depending on the number of clusters he chooses to see. After all, K-means clustering is a method for unsupervised learning. The goal of this technique is to find groups in the data based on inherent feature similarity. It allows to find groups that have formed organically.
